\section{Definitions}

We propose the following addition to the module language syntax:

\begin{figure}[h]
    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{r c ll}
            $P$         & ::= & $X$                                                                                                                              \\
                        & |   & $X.P$                                                                                                                            \\[3mm]
            $\pi$       & ::= & $\epsilon$                                                                                                                       \\
                        & |   & $P$                                                                                                                              \\[3mm]
            $M$         & ::= & $P[\overline{\tx = t}]$         & \color{Gray} local variable with module type  (in practice: $X\; |\; X.P[\overline{\tx = t}]$) \\
                        & |   & $\Elixir.P[\overline{\tx = t}]$ & \color{Gray} global module                                                                     \\
                        & |   & $\Self$                         & \color{Gray} self-reference (syntax $\texttt{\_\_MODULE\_\_}$ in Elixir)                       \\
                        & |   & $\Self.P[\overline{\tx = t}]$   & \color{Gray} inside self-reference                                                             \\[3mm]
            $\Next$     & ::= & $N$                                                                                                                              \\
                        & |   & $\tx$                                                                                                                            \\[3mm]
            $\Gammatop$ & ::= & $\overline{X : \Moddecl}$       & \color{Gray} global environment                                                                \\[3mm]
            $\Gammabot$ & ::= & $\overline{D}$                  & \color{Gray} local environment ($D \neq X : \Moddecl$)
        \end{tabular}
    \end{center}
    \caption{Some addition to the module language syntax}
\end{figure}

$\Gammatop$ is the global environment. It contains top-level \texttt{defmodule} declarations.

$\Gammabot$ is the local environment. It cannot contain \texttt{defmodule} declarations.

Empty environments will be denoted $\emptyenv$. \\

By abuse of notation, $X.\epsilon$ and $X$ will denote the same thing.
This will allow us to write $X.\pi$ where $\pi$ is a possibly empty path. \\

We consider behavior environments and declaration lists as partial functions
from $\BX$ and $\Next$ to $\Beh$ and $D$, respectively.
If the name appears multiple time in the list, the right-most occurrence is returned, as one would expect.

We extend the notation to paths:

\begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{rll}
        $(\overline{D})(X.P)$          & $=$ & $(\overline{D})(X)(P)$ \\
        $\left(X : \Moddecl\right)(P)$ & $=$ & $(\overline{D})(P)$
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{Wellformedness}

We introduce wellformedness judgements:

\begin{figure}[H]
    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{lrll}
            Behavior environments: &                                     & $\vdash \Sigma$                                                                                  \\[2mm]
            Global environments:   & $\Sigma$                            & $\vdash \Gammatop$                                                                               \\[2mm]
            Local environments:    & $\pi; \Sigma; \Gammatop$            & $\vdash \Gammabot$    & \color{Gray} $\pi = \epsilon$ implies $\Gammabot = \emptyenv$            \\[2mm]
            Declaration lists:     & $\pi; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot$ & $\vdash \overline{D}$ & \color{Gray} $\pi \neq \epsilon$ except for top level \texttt{defmodule} \\[2mm]
            Type:                  & $P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot$   & $\vdash T$
        \end{tabular}
    \end{center}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Behavior environments}

For behaviour environments we extend the syntax of the names so as to include the name \Self{} (corresponding to the \texttt{\_\_MODULE\_\_} variable in Elixir).

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \infer0{ \vdash \emptyenv }
    \end{prooftree}
    \qquad
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ \vdash \Sigma }
        \hypo{ \overline{\tx} \text{ pairwise distinct} }
        \hypo{ \Self\color{black}; \Sigma; (\Self:\Lambda().\{\}); \overline{\tx : \star} \vdash \overline D }
        \infer3{ \vdash \Sigma, \BX : \Lambda (\overline{\tx : \star}).\{\overline{D}\} }
    \end{prooftree}
\]

\subsection{Global environments}
\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo { \vdash \Sigma }
        \infer1 { \Sigma \vdash \emptyenv }
    \end{prooftree}
\]

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{\Sigma \vdash \Gammatop}
        \hypo{ \epsilon; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \emptyenv \vdash X : \Moddecl }
        \infer2{ \Sigma \vdash \Gammatop, X : \Moddecl }
    \end{prooftree}
\]

In the rule above, $ \epsilon; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \emptyenv \vdash X : \Moddecl$
refers to the wellformedness of declaration lists.

\subsection{Local type environments}

We define the function $\name(D)$ as $\name: \left\{\begin{array}{ll}
        X : \Moddecl             & \mapsto X   \\
        X : t                    & \mapsto X   \\
        x : \bigcap \overline{T} & \mapsto x   \\
        \tx : \star              & \mapsto \tx \\
        \tx : [=t]               & \mapsto \tx
    \end{array}\right.$  \\[3mm]

We also define the notation $\left(\overline{D}\right)[\pi \texttt{+=} D']$ as:

\begin{figure}[H]
    \begin{tabular}{lll}
        $\left(\overline{D}\right)[\epsilon \texttt{+=} D']$                                 & = $\overline{D}, D$                                                                          \\[3mm]
        $\left(\overline{D}, X : \Moddecl[\tx][\overline{D'}]\right)[X.\pi \texttt{+=} D'']$ & = $\overline{D}, X : \Moddecl[\tx][\left(\overline{D'}\right)[\pi \texttt{+=} D'']]$         \\[3mm]
        $\left(\overline{D}, D'\right)[X.\pi \texttt{+=} D'']$                               & $= \left(\overline{D}\right)[X.\pi \texttt{+=} D''], D'$ \hspace*{15mm}if $\name(D') \neq X$ \\[3mm]
        undefined otherwise
    \end{tabular}
    \caption{Notation $\left(\overline{D}\right)[\pi \texttt{+=} D']$}
\end{figure}

Note that we may only add a declaration to a $X: \Moddecl$, and this module must already be present in the declaration list.

The above notations and definitions allow us to propose the following rules for well-formedness of type environments.
Recall the shape of the judgement: $\pi; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot$.

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ \Sigma \vdash \Gammatop}
        \infer1{ \pi; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \emptyenv }
    \end{prooftree}
\]


\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ (P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot)? }
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash t }
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot, X : t }
    \end{prooftree}
\]
\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot }
        \infer1{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot, \tx : \star }
    \end{prooftree}
\]
\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ (P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot)? }
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash t }
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot, \tx : [= t] }
    \end{prooftree}
\]
\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot }
        \hypo{ \overline{P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash T} }
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot, x : \bigcap \overline{T} }
    \end{prooftree}
\] \\

Note that we have no rule to add $X : \Moddecl$ to $\Gammabot$. Therefore, for what concerns module declarations, a local type environment $\Gammabot$ cannot contain module declaration but only type assumptions for module parameters.
Also, as stated above, note that all the rules requires a non-empty path $P$ except for the base case $\emptyenv$.

\subsection{Declaration list}

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ \pi; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot }
        \infer1{ \pi; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \emptyenv }
    \end{prooftree}
\]

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop[P \texttt{+=} D]; (\Gammabot, D) \vdash \overline{D'} }
        \hypo{ \name(D) \notin \dom(\Gammabot) }
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash D, \overline{D'} }
    \end{prooftree}
\]

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ \pi; \Sigma; \Gammatop\left[\pi \texttt{+=} X : \Moddecl\right]; \Gammabot \vdash \overline{D'} }
        \hypo{ \pi.X; \Sigma; \Gammatop\left[\pi \texttt{+=} X : \Lambda (\overline{\tx : \star}). \{ \}\right]; \overline{\tx : \star} \vdash \{ \overline{D} \}_{\overline{\BX[\overline{\tx' = t}]}}}
        \infer2{ \pi; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \left(X : \Moddecl\right), \overline{D'} }
    \end{prooftree}
\]
The above rule also requires the $\overline{\tx} \text{ are pairwise distinct} $.

\subsection{Pretypes}

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo { P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot }
        \infer1{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \tau }
    \end{prooftree}
    \qquad
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ \overline{P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash t}}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash t'}
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash (\overline{t}) \rightarrow t' }
    \end{prooftree}
    \qquad
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot }
        \hypo{ \overline{P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash t} }
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash  \%\{ \overline{k: t} \} }
    \end{prooftree}
\]

Keys $\overline{k}$ also need to be pairwise distinct. \\

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash t }
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash t' }
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash  t \vee  t' }
    \end{prooftree}
    \qquad
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash t }
        \infer1{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash  \lnot t }
    \end{prooftree}
\]

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop \vdash \Gammabot }
        \hypo{ \tx \in \dom(\Gammabot) }
        \infer2{P;  \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \tx }
    \end{prooftree}
    \qquad
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo { \overline {P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash t} }
        \hypo { \Sigma(\BX) = \BX : \Lambda \left( \overline{\tx : \star} \right) \{\overline D \}}
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \BX [\overline{\tx = t}]  }
    \end{prooftree}
\]

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash M : \{ \tx : \star \} }
        \infer1{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash M.\tx }
    \end{prooftree}
\]

In the above rule, $P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash M : \{ \tx : \star \}$ is a typing judgement.
Thanks to subtyping, $\{ \tx : \star \}$ may not be exactly the type associated with $M$ in $\Gammatop$ or $\Gammabot$.

\section{Expressions}

We define the expression typing judgement: $P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash E : T$.

Because the syntactic intersection is not considered as a type, we also introduce the pseudo-judgement:
$P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash E : \bigcap \overline{T}$ and the trivial pseudo-subtyping rule:

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash E : \bigcap \overline{T} }
        \hypo{ T' \in \overline{T} }
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash E: T'}
    \end{prooftree}
\] \\


\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash E : T }
        \hypo{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash T \preceq T' }
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash E: T' }
    \end{prooftree}
\] \\

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo { P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \overline{k : t} }
        \hypo { \overline{P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash E : t} }
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \%\{\overline{k = E}\} : \%\{\overline{k : t}\} }
    \end{prooftree}
\] \\

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo { P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \overline{(\overline{t}) \rightarrow t'} }
        \hypo { \overline{P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot, \overline{x : t} \vdash E: t'} }
        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \overline{(\overline{t}) \rightarrow t'} \text{ fn } \overline{x} \rightarrow E : \bigwedge \overline{ (\overline{t}) \rightarrow t'} }
    \end{prooftree}
\] \\

\[
    \begin{prooftree}
        \hypo { P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \overline{(\overline{N : T}) \rightarrow T'} }

        \infer2{ P; \Sigma; \Gammatop; \Gammabot \vdash \bigcap \overline{(\overline{N : T}) \rightarrow T'} \text{ fn } \overline{N} \rightarrow E : \bigcap \overline{(\overline{N : T }) \rightarrow T'} }
    \end{prooftree}
\] \\

\[

    \end{prooftree}
\] \\
